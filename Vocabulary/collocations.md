# 🔗 Collocations

> A collocation is a pair or group of words that are habitually used together.  
> Learning collocations sounds more natural than single vocabulary words.

---

## Verb + Noun

| Verb | Noun | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| **make** | a decision, a mistake, an effort, progress, sense | *She made a tremendous effort to finish on time.* |
| **do** | research, damage, harm, good, business | *The storm did considerable damage to the crops.* |
| **take** | a risk, responsibility, action, notes, a break | *It's time to take responsibility for your choices.* |
| **give** | advice, a speech, permission, a hand, feedback | *Could you give me some feedback on my essay?* |
| **have** | a conversation, an impact, doubts, potential | *The project has enormous potential.* |
| **reach** | a conclusion, an agreement, a goal, a deadline | *They finally reached an agreement after hours of talks.* |
| **raise** | awareness, concerns, questions, funds | *The campaign raised awareness about mental health.* |

---

## Adjective + Noun

| Adjective | Noun | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| strong | evidence, argument, influence | *There's strong evidence that sleep affects memory.* |
| heavy | traffic, rain, workload, smoker | *The heavy workload left her exhausted.* |
| high | expectations, priority, risk, demand | *The product is in high demand.* |
| deep | concern, understanding, impact | *Her death had a deep impact on the community.* |
| broad | range, knowledge, support | *The policy has broad public support.* |

---

## Adverb + Adjective (intensifiers)

| Adverb | Adjective | ✗ Avoid |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| deeply | concerned, moved, committed | ~~very concerned~~ |
| highly | unlikely, recommended, skilled | ~~very unlikely~~ |
| utterly | exhausted, confused, wrong | ~~very wrong~~ |
| bitterly | disappointed, cold, regret | ~~very disappointed~~ |
| widely | known, used, accepted | ~~very known~~ |

## B2 Exam Useful Collocations

- *raise/address/tackle* a problem
- *meet/exceed/fall short of* expectations
- *draw/reach/come to* a conclusion
- *have/reach/make* a compromise
- *play a crucial/vital/key role* in...
