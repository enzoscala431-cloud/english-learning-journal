## Entries

### inevitable — adjective
**Definition:** certain to happen; impossible to avoid  
**Source example:** "Climate change makes some coastal flooding inevitable." — *BBC News*  
**Other example:** When you don't practice regularly, forgetting vocabulary is inevitable.  
**Notes:** syn: unavoidable, inescapable | collocates with: *seem*, *appear*, *feel* inevitable

---

### nuance — noun
**Definition:** a subtle difference in meaning, expression, or sound  
**Source example:** "The translator struggled to capture the nuances of the original text." — *The Guardian*  
**Other example:** Learning idioms helps you understand the nuances of everyday conversation.  
**Notes:** often plural: *nuances of* | adj form: *nuanced* (a nuanced argument)

---

### reluctant — adjective
**Definition:** unwilling to do something; hesitant  
**Source example:** "She was reluctant to admit she'd been wrong." — *The Economist*  
**Other example:** I'm often reluctant to speak up in meetings, even when I know the answer.  
**Notes:** pattern: *reluctant + to + infinitive* | adv: reluctantly

---

### forthcoming — adjective
**Definition:** (1) about to happen/appear; (2) willing to give information  
**Source example:** "The forthcoming report is expected to be controversial." — *Reuters*  
**Other example:** He wasn't very forthcoming about his reasons for leaving.  
**Notes:** Two distinct meanings — context is everything!

---

### drawback — noun  
**Definition:** a disadvantage or problem  
**Source example:** "The main drawback of remote work is isolation." — *Harvard Business Review*  
**Other example:** The only drawback of living in Mendoza is the lack of direct international flights.  
**Notes:** syn: downside, disadvantage | opposite: benefit, advantage | *the main/only drawback*

### [Coastal] — [adjetive]
**Definition:** Located on, near, or relating to the coast (the land next to the sea).
**Source example:** "Coastal towns are preparing for the upcoming storm" — *Cambridge Dictionary*
**Other example:** The new shipping facility will be built in a coastal area to make international transport easier.
**Notes:**synonyms: seaside / collocations: coastal waters, coastal erosion 

---
### [Flooding] — [noun]
**Definition:** A situation in which an area of land that is normally dry becomes covered with water, usually after heavy rain.
**Source example:** "The heavy rain caused severe flooding in the city center."
**Other example:** We had to pause the factory operations because the flooding damaged the main warehouse.
**Notes:** synonyms: inundation / collocations: severe flooding, risk of flooding

---
### [Struggle] — [verd]
**Definition:** To experience difficulty and make a very great effort in order to achieve something or deal with a problem.
**Source example:** "The company has struggled to maintain its profits this year." 
**Other example:** The maintenance team struggled to repair the machine before the morning shift started.
**Notes:** synonyms have a hard time, fight / collocations: struggle with a problem, struggle to understand

---
### [unwilling] - [adjective]
**Definition:**Not wanting to do something and refusing to do it.
**Source example:** "The banks were completely unwilling to lend the company more money." 
**Other example:** The supervisor was unwilling to approve the project without a proper safety analysis.
**Notes:** synonyms: reluctant, opposed / collocations: unwilling to compromise, unwilling to accept / register: formal / neutral

---
### [hesitant] — [adjective]
**Definition:** Not doing something immediately or quickly because you are nervous, uncertain, or doubtful.
**Source example:** "She was hesitant about accepting the new job offer."
**Other example:** The operators were hesitant to use the new control panel because they hadn't received enough training yet.
**Notes:** synonyms: uncertain, unsure / collocations: hesitant to make a decision, feel hesitant

---

### [lack] — [noun]
**Definition:** The state of not having something, or not having enough of something. (Can also be used as a verb).
**Source example:**  "The project was cancelled due to a lack of funding."
**Other example:** A lack of clear communication between departments can cause serious delays in production
**Notes:** synonyms shortage, absence / collocations: lack of experience, complete lack of
