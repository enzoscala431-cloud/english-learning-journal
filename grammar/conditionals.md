# 🔀 Conditionals

> English has 4 main conditionals + mixed conditionals at B2/C1 level.

---

## The 4 Main Conditionals

### Zero — General truths
**If + present simple → present simple**

> Used for: facts, scientific truths, habits

- *If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.*
- *If I drink coffee after 8pm, I can't sleep.*

---

### First — Real future possibility
**If + present simple → will + infinitive**

> Used for: likely future events, warnings, promises

- *If it rains tomorrow, we'll cancel the picnic.*
- *If you study consistently, you'll improve fast.*
- *If you don't submit by Friday, you won't get credit.*

⚠️ Never use *will* in the *if* clause: ~~If it will rain...~~

---

### Second — Unreal present / unlikely future
**If + past simple → would + infinitive**

> Used for: hypothetical situations, advice, wishes, dreams

- *If I had more time, I would learn Japanese.*
- *If I were you, I'd apologize.* (note: *were*, not *was*, in formal English)
- *What would you do if you won the lottery?*

---

### Third — Unreal past (regret / speculation)
**If + past perfect → would have + past participle**

> Used for: imagining different past outcomes, regret

- *If I had studied harder, I would have passed.*
- *If she hadn't missed the train, she wouldn't have been late.*
- *I would have called you if I had known.*

---

## Mixed Conditionals (B2/C1)

### Past cause → Present result
**If + past perfect → would + infinitive**

- *If I had taken that job, I would be living in London now.*
- *If she hadn't dropped out, she'd be a doctor today.*

### Present cause → Past result
**If + past simple → would have + past participle**

- *If I weren't so stubborn, I would have asked for help.*

---

## Other Conditional Expressions

| Expression | Use | Example |
|-----------|-----|---------|
| **Unless** | = if not | *Unless you hurry, we'll be late.* |
| **Provided / As long as** | = if and only if | *I'll come, provided you invite her too.* |
| **In case** | = as a precaution | *Take an umbrella in case it rains.* |
| **Suppose / What if** | hypothetical | *Suppose you lost your job — what would you do?* |
| **Were it not for** | formal / literary | *Were it not for your help, I'd have failed.* |

---

## Common B2 Mistakes

| ✗ Wrong | ✓ Correct |
|---------|-----------|
| *If I would have money...* | If I **had** money... |
| *If I was you...* | If I **were** you... (formal) |
| *If it will stop raining...* | If it **stops** raining... |
| *I would went* | I would **have gone** |
