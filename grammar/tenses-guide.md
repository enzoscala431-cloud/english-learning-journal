# ⏰ Tenses Guide

> The most common source of errors at B2 level. Focus on contrast and context.

---

## The Big Confusions at B2

### Past Simple vs Present Perfect

| Past Simple | Present Perfect |
|-------------|----------------|
| Finished time reference: *yesterday, last week, in 2020, when I was a child* | No time reference OR: *already, yet, ever, never, just, recently* |
| Focus on the **event** | Focus on the **result / experience** |
| *She **lived** in Paris for two years.* (she doesn't anymore) | *She **has lived** in Paris for two years.* (she still does) |
| *Did you eat?* (specific meal we're talking about) | *Have you ever eaten sushi?* (in your whole life) |

**Common error:** ~~*I have seen him yesterday.*~~ → I saw him yesterday. ✓  
(Yesterday = specific past time → past simple)

---

### Past Continuous vs Past Simple

- Past continuous = **background action** (was happening)
- Past simple = **interrupting action** (happened)

> *I **was working** on my laptop when the power suddenly **went** out.*

| Use | Form | Example |
|-----|------|---------|
| Background/interrupted | was/were + -ing | *She was cooking when I arrived.* |
| Simultaneous ongoing actions | was/were + -ing ... was/were + -ing | *While I was studying, she was sleeping.* |
| Habit in the past (with *always*) | was/were + always + -ing | *He was always losing his keys.* |

---

### Past Perfect — When and Why

Use it when you have **two past events** and you need to show which happened first.

> *When I arrived at the party, most people **had already left**.*  
> (They left → THEN I arrived — past perfect shows the earlier event)

Only use past perfect when the sequence matters. If it's obvious from context, past simple alone is fine.

---

### Present Perfect Continuous

**Form:** have/has + been + verb-ing

**Use:** An action that started in the past and is **still happening** OR **just stopped** with a visible result

> *I'**ve been studying** English for 3 years.* (still studying)  
> *Your eyes are red — **have you been crying**?* (just stopped, visible result)

**Contrast:**
- *I've read that book.* → I finished it (result = knowledge)
- *I've been reading that book.* → I'm in the middle of it (ongoing process)

---

### Future Forms

| Form | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **will** | Predictions, decisions at moment of speaking | *It'll be fine. / I'll have the salad.* |
| **going to** | Intentions, evidence-based predictions | *I'm going to apply for the job. / Look at those clouds — it's going to rain.* |
| **Present continuous** | Fixed arrangements | *I'm meeting her at 6.* |
| **Present simple** | Timetables, schedules | *The train leaves at 9.* |
| **will + have + pp** | Future perfect: completed before a future moment | *By Friday, I will have finished the report.* |

## Quick Self-Test

Fill in the correct tense:

1. By the time she arrives, I ___ (finish) cooking.
2. He ___ (work) in that company since 2019.
3. I ___ (just / send) you the file.
4. When I woke up, it ___ (snow) all night.

**Answers:** 1. will have finished | 2. has been working | 3. have just sent | 4. had been snowing
