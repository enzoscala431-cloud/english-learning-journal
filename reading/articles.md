### "The hidden costs of remote work" — Harvard Business Review — [date]
**Topic:** Work culture, productivity  
**Difficulty:** B2  
**New vocabulary:** proximity bias, asynchronous, burnout, attrition, productivity paradox  
**Summary:**
The article argues that while remote work offers flexibility, it carries significant hidden costs for employees' career progression. Workers who are not physically present in the office often suffer from proximity bias — managers tend to promote people they see regularly. The author also notes that the blurring of work-home boundaries increases burnout rates. The piece concludes that hybrid models work best when companies establish clear norms around visibility and communication.

**Opinion:**
This resonates with my experience. The point about proximity bias is particularly compelling — it's easy to overlook employees you don't interact with face-to-face. However, I think the article underestimates how much remote work benefits workers in regions with poor local job markets.
